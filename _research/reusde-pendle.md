Research on reUSDe zero coupon bond strategies via Pendle.

## Overview

Pendle allows tokenizing yield-bearing assets into Principal Tokens (PT) and Yield Tokens (YT). This research explores fixed income opportunities with reUSDe on Pendle.

## How It Works

1. **Principal Token (PT)**: Represents the principal, redeemable for underlying at maturity
2. **Yield Token (YT)**: Represents the yield generated until maturity
3. **Buying PT**: Locks in a fixed rate (zero coupon bond strategy)
4. **Buying YT**: Speculates on yield being higher than implied

## Strategies

### Fixed Rate (Buy PT)
- Lock in fixed yield by purchasing PT at discount
- Hold until maturity for guaranteed return
- Risk: Opportunity cost if floating rates exceed fixed rate

### Yield Speculation (Buy YT)
- Profit if actual yield exceeds implied yield
- Higher risk, higher potential reward
- Time decay as maturity approaches

### LP Strategy
- Provide liquidity to PT/underlying pool
- Earn trading fees + incentives
- Impermanent loss considerations

## Risks

- **Smart Contract Risk**: Pendle and underlying protocol risk
- **Liquidity Risk**: Ability to exit position before maturity
- **Underlying Risk**: reUSDe depeg or yield changes
- **Maturity Risk**: PT only redeemable at face value at maturity

## Key Metrics to Track

- Fixed APY vs floating APY spread
- Time to maturity
- Pool liquidity depth
- Historical yield volatility
